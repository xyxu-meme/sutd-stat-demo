Both panels share the same x-axis and y-axis, so you can directly compare where z sits vs. where x = σz + μ sits on the same number line. The standard normal (top) is always a unit bell at 0. The general normal (bottom) shifts with μ and widens or narrows with σ, changing its peak height too — but because width and height trade off (1/σ), the total area stays 1, and equal-probability regions stay equal.

Click ▶ Standardize: the bottom curve shifts to center 0, then squeezes to width 1, until it exactly overlaps the top curve. The shaded area never changes. That overlap is the proof.

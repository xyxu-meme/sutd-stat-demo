Drag the orange dot, or type an exact x above. The view zooms automatically to fit the distribution.

The curve isn't drawn from the histogram — it's a separate object, the Normal(p, p(1−p)/n) distribution, with its own pdf and its own integral (via the standard erf-based normal cdf). Where amber and sage overlap you'll see a blended tone; where they disagree, one color pokes past the other. At small n that mismatch is visible; by n ≈ 100–200 it mostly vanishes — the De Moivre–Laplace approximation (the historical root of the Central Limit Theorem) closing the gap between a sum and an integral.

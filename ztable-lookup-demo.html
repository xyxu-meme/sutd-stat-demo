Standard Normal Table  —  Φ(z) for z ≤ 0 For z > 0: use Φ(z) = 1 − Φ(−z)

The Z-table lists Φ(z) = P(Z ≤ z) for negative z-values. Each row gives the first two significant digits of z (e.g. −1.2), and each column gives the last decimal digit (e.g. .03). Together they identify a unique z = −1.23.

For positive z, use the symmetry property: Φ(z) = 1 − Φ(−z). Look up −z in the table, then subtract from 1.

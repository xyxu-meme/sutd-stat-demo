pdf ↔ cdf: Area Becomes Height

Drag x and watch one number show up two ways: as area under f(x), and as height on F(x). They're always equal — that's the whole definition of a cdf.

pdf — f(x)height = density, area = probability
cdf — F(x)height = probability, directly
x =
a, b = to
f(x)  |  F(x) = P(X ≤ x) —
P(a < X ≤ b) = F(b) − F(a) —

Drag on either chart to move x — both panels stay in sync.

The shaded region under f(x) from the left edge up to x is, by definition, F(x) — that's what F(x) = ∫f(t)dt means. Turn on Show slope to see the reverse direction: the steepness of F at x always matches the height of f at x, since F′(x) = f(x).

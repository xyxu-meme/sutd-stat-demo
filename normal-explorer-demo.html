To compute probabilities with any normal distribution, standardize first: convert X-values to Z = (X − μ) / σ, then look up Φ(z). The readout below shows every step. Drag the handles to set an interval P(a ≤ X ≤ b), or switch to Point mode to see the cdf P(X ≤ x) = Φ(z) directly.

The 68-95-99.7 rule says roughly 68.3% of the probability lies within ±1σ of the mean, 95.5% within ±2σ, and 99.7% within ±3σ. These percentages are universal — they don’t change when you adjust μ or σ.

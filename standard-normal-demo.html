For the standard normal Z ∼ N(0, 1), we use special notation: φ (lowercase phi) is the pdf — the height of the bell curve. Φ (uppercase Phi) is the cdf — the area from −∞ to x, equal to P(Z ≤ x).

In Point mode, drag or type x to see Φ(x). In Interval mode, drag two handles to see P(a ≤ Z ≤ b) = Φ(b) − Φ(a). Toggle Symmetry (point mode only) to see Φ(−x) = 1 − Φ(x).
